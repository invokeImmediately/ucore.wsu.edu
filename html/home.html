What are UCORE (University Common Requirements) classes?

These general education classes are a key WSU graduation requirement. They ensure that students received a well-rounded education through a curriculum that is shared by all students to meet the learning goals and outcomes of a WSU baccalaureate degree.

The Importance of a Well-Rounded Education

"So what does business need from our educational system? One answer is that it needs more employees who excel in science and engineering and the remainder of a workforce that is exposed to enough science and mathematics to function in the rapidly evolving high-tech world.

"But that is only the beginning: one cannot live by equations alone. The need is increasing for workers with greater foreign language skills and an expanded knowledge of economics, history and geography. And who wants a technology-driven economy when those who drive it are not grounded in such fields as ethics?"

—Norman Augustine, former Chairman and CEO of the Lockheed Martin Corporation, 2013

"Liberal education is not a static tradition of learning. Rather, it is and always has been a form of education that is richly and generatively engaged with the life and needs of the larger society… Together, we are finding more powerful ways to teach students how to make sense of complexity; how to find, evaluate, and use new evidence; and how to apply their knowledge to real problems. While traditionally we have thought of these analytical and inquiry capacities as 'intellectual powers,' the reality is that, in today's knowledge-fueled society, they are deeply practical skills as well."

—Dr. Carol Geary Schneider, Past President, AAC&U
