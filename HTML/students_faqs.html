Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know whether or not a course meets a UCORE requirement?

UCORE classes are all identified by a bracket and a 4 digit code in the WSU course catalog indicating which requirement the class satsifies.

[ROOT] = Roots of Contemporary Issues

[QUAN] = Quantitative Reasoning

[WRTG] = Written Communication

[COMM] = Communication

[SSCI] = Inquiry in the Social Sciences

[HUM] = Inquiry in the Humanities

[ARTS] = Inquiry in the Creative and Professional Arts

[BSCI], [PSCI], & [SCI] = Inquiry in the Natural Sciences

[DIVR] = Diversity

[CAPS] = Integrative Capstone

How many UCORE classes can be taken within my major?

A maximum of three (3- or 4-credit) UCORE courses may be taken within your majorAdditional Notes|A UCORE course is within your major if it is offered by the same academic department/unit that administers your major. For example, if you are a mathematics major, then any class within the WSU catalog that has a course prefix of MATH is considered within your major.. For the purpose of this limitation, three 1-credit courses may be combined to count for a single 3-credit course.

Can my UCORE courses be taken pass/fail?

No course designated as a University Common Requirement (UCORE) can be taken on a pass, fail basis. All UCORE-designated courses must be letter-graded (i.e., A, B, C, D, and F). The only exception possible is for CAPS courses, which may have S, F grading.

How many Communication Proficiency requirements are there?

Two courses in Written/Communication Competencies are required. One must be [WRTG] typically fulfilled with English 101 or the student's placement writing course. The second course can be either [COMM], or a second [WRTG] course.

What about Honors Students?

Honors students complete the Honors College requirements outlined in the Honors section of the WSU course catalog.

Are Writing in the Major (or "[M]") courses required in the UCORE curriculum?

Two [M] courses must be taken to satisfy a University-wide graduation requirement, although they are not specifically part of the UCORE curriculum. Speaking of graduation requirements, earning a degree at WSU requires:

  • Completing all UCORE requirements
  • Passing two [M] courses
  • Earning 40 upper-division credits
  • Earning 120 total credits
  • Obtaining a final cumulative GPA of at least 2.0
  • Completing the writing portfolio
  • Satisfying all major and college requirements
What are the Science requirements?

The Science requirements are a little more complex than other UCORE requirements; here's how they work. Firstly, students must take a minimum of 7 credits. However, students with majors administered by the College of Arts and Sciences must take a minimum of 8 credits. Secondly, and regardless of major, one course must be in the biological sciences [BSCI] and one in the physical sciences [PSCI]. Additionally, at least one of those two courses must also include a lab. However, students with majors administered by the College of Arts and Sciences must take two lab courses. Finally, Science 101 [SCI] and Science 102 [SCI] with two labs (8 credits) will substitute for the aforementioned [BSCI] and [PSCI] courses.
